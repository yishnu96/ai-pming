# AI PMing — Brand Brief

> This file is the single source of truth for who we are, who we serve, and how we communicate. Every piece of content — blog posts, docs, social, emails — must align with this brief.

---

## Who We Are

**AI PMing** is an educational platform that makes AI accessible to everyone — especially non-tech professionals who feel left behind by the AI revolution. We bridge the gap between "AI sounds cool" and "I use AI every day at work."

We are not a coding bootcamp. We are not an academic journal. We are the friend who sat down with you over coffee and made AI click.

---

## Mission

**Make AI understandable and usable for every professional, regardless of their technical background.**

We believe:
- AI literacy is the new computer literacy — everyone needs it
- You don't need to code to use AI effectively
- Learning should feel like a conversation, not a lecture
- Real examples beat theory every time

## Target Audience

### Primary: The AI-Curious Professional
- **Who:** Working professionals aged 25-50 — product managers, marketers, sales leaders, CEOs, teachers, HR professionals, consultants, small business owners
- **Tech level:** Low to moderate. They use apps daily but don't code. They've heard of ChatGPT but haven't explored beyond basic prompts
- **Mindset:** Curious but overwhelmed. They know AI matters but don't know where to start. They fear being left behind
- **Pain points:**
  - "I don't understand what AI actually is"
  - "Everyone talks about AI but I don't know how to use it for MY work"
  - "I tried ChatGPT once but got mediocre results"
  - "Technical articles lose me in the first paragraph"
- **What they want:** Practical, jargon-free guidance they can apply today. Not theory — results

### Secondary: Students & Career Changers
- **Who:** College students, MBA candidates, professionals pivoting into tech-adjacent roles
- **What they want:** Foundational AI knowledge to be competitive in the job market

### Who We Are NOT For
- Data scientists or ML engineers looking for advanced technical content
- Researchers looking for academic papers
- People who want to learn to code AI models from scratch

## Voice & Tone

### Our Voice (consistent across all content)
- **Knowledgeable but approachable** — We know our stuff, but we never talk down to people
- **Direct and clear** — Every sentence earns its place. No fluff, no filler
- **Warm and encouraging** — Learning something new is hard. We acknowledge that and cheer people forward
- **Practical above all** — We don't explain for the sake of explaining. We explain so you can DO

### Our Tone (adapts by context)

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Explaining a concept | Patient, uses everyday analogies, like a good teacher |
| Tool guides & tutorials | Energetic, step-by-step, "let's do this together" |
| Profession-specific guides | Empathetic to the role's challenges, solution-focused |
| Comparisons & roundups | Honest, opinionated but fair, no fake neutrality |

### Writing Rules
- Use "you" and "your" — speak directly to the reader
- Write at a grade 6-8 reading level (smart 12-year-old can follow)
- Sentences under 20 words on average
- Paragraphs max 3-4 sentences
- NO jargon without an immediate plain-English explanation and example
- NO condescending words: "simply", "just", "obviously", "as everyone knows"
- NO empty hype: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "cutting-edge" (unless backed by evidence)
- YES to everyday analogies: cooking, driving, organizing, shopping
- YES to "For example..." — concrete beats abstract, always

## Content Principles (derived from this brief)

1. **One article, one outcome** — Every piece teaches exactly one thing the reader can do or understand
2. **Start where they are** — Open with what's familiar, then bridge to what's new
3. **Show, don't lecture** — Examples first, theory second
4. **Respect their time** — If it can be said in fewer words, say it in fewer words
5. **Honest and grounded** — No fabricated claims, no hype, no overclaiming. If we don't know, we say so
6. **Always connect to their work** — Every concept links back to how it applies in a real professional context
