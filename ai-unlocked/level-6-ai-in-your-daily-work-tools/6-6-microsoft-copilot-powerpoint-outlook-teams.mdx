## Slides from Scratch

Open PowerPoint. Click the Copilot button in the ribbon. Type: **"Create a 5-slide overview of our Q2 marketing results."**

Copilot generates a structured deck — titles, bullet points, suggested layouts. You can also feed it a Word document and ask: **"Turn this report into a presentation."** It reads your document and builds slide-by-slide content.

Not happy with the design? Click **"Redesign this slide"** and Copilot suggests cleaner layouts, better visuals, or a different format.

**Quick wins in PowerPoint:**
- Generate a first-draft deck from a single sentence
- Convert a Word doc into structured slides
- Redesign cluttered slides with one click
- Add speaker notes automatically

**The limitation:** Copilot gives you a solid first draft. It does not add complex animations, brand-specific templates, or data visualizations that need manual tweaking. That's still on you.

## Inbox on Autopilot

Open Outlook. Select a long email thread. Hit the Copilot button.

Copilot summarizes the thread into **decisions made, open questions, and action items with owners.** No more scrolling through twenty replies.

Need to reply? Type **"Help me respond professionally"** and Copilot drafts a reply based on the thread context and your tone preferences — formal, casual, or brief.

**Two things Copilot does in Outlook:**
1. **Summarizes threads** — saves the most scrolling
2. **Drafts replies** — saves the most thinking

**The contrast:** Copilot does not copy-paste templates. You set the tone, provide the facts, it composes, and you review before sending. You stay in control.

## Meetings Captured

This is where Copilot in Teams shines. Join a meeting, then open the Copilot panel afterward.

You get a structured recap with four sections:

1. **Summary** — what the meeting was about
2. **Decisions** — what was agreed upon
3. **Action items** — who is doing what by when
4. **Timeline** — timestamp-linked moments you can click to replay

Running late? Type **"What did I miss?"** and Copilot catches you up on the part you skipped.

## Put It Together

Picture your Monday morning:

**9:00 AM — Outlook.** Copilot summarizes a 15-email thread from the week. Three decisions, two questions, one action assigned to you. You draft a reply in 30 seconds.

**10:00 AM — Teams.** You join the project kickoff late. "What did I miss?" catches you up. When the meeting ends, Copilot hands you action items with names and deadlines.

**2:00 PM — PowerPoint.** You need a status deck for tomorrow. Tell Copilot your goal. A first draft appears. You tweak three slides and add your own data.

**Here is the critical contrast:** Copilot is a first-draft machine. It is not a replacement for your judgment. Every output needs your review — factual accuracy, tone, and context are yours to own.
