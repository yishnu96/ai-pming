## What Is Copilot

Copilot is the natural-language interface built into Microsoft Office. You type or say what you need in plain English, and it works directly inside the app you are already using.

Think of it like asking a colleague who already knows your data. You say "show me the trend," and it builds the chart. You say "make this sound more formal," and it rewrites the paragraph.

Here is the important part you need to understand right away. Copilot is **not** a standalone chatbot you open in your browser. It lives inside the apps — Excel, Word, PowerPoint, Outlook. It works on *your* files with *your* data.

:::info Think About It
Where does Copilot run — as a website, or inside your apps?

*Inside the apps — Excel, Word, PowerPoint, Outlook.*
:::

And Excel is where it gets impressive. Excel formulas frustrated people for decades. VLOOKUP, IF, INDEX-MATCH — entire careers were built on remembering them. Copilot removes that barrier entirely.

## Excel Magic

### Formulas in Plain English

You no longer need to memorize Excel formulas. Ask Copilot what you want.

Type: *"Find the region with the highest Q3 sales."* Copilot writes the formula for you — and explains how it works if you want to understand it.

But Copilot does not stop at writing formulas. It creates entire formula columns and rows based on your existing data. You have a column of dates and a column of revenue? Ask: *"Calculate the month-over-month growth rate."* Copilot fills in the new column with the right formulas.

And if you are not sure what a formula you already found online actually does? Paste it into Copilot and ask it to explain. It breaks it down in plain English.

**What Copilot generates is a real, editable Excel formula.** It is not a static answer. Change the underlying numbers and the formula recalculates like any other Excel formula.

:::info Think About It
Besides writing formulas, what else can Copilot do with your data in Excel?

*Analyze trends, find outliers, create charts and PivotTables — all without writing a single formula.*
:::

### Analyze Your Data

Excel has a feature called **Analyze Data** that runs trends, patterns, and outliers through your spreadsheet — all triggered by natural language.

Try asking:
- *"What are the key trends in this sales data?"*
- *"Show me outliers in these expense numbers."*
- *"Compare Q1 and Q2 performance by region."*

Copilot returns summaries, PivotTables, and charts you can click through.

### Charts That Work

Tell Copilot: *"Create a bar chart showing monthly revenue for 2025."* Within seconds, it generates a chart you can edit, resize, and reformat. These are fully functional Excel charts — not static images. Change a number in your sheet and the chart updates automatically.

Copilot isn't just about numbers. It writes prose too.

## Word Superpowers

### Draft from Scratch

Tell Copilot what you need and it writes the first draft.

Type: *"Draft a professional email proposing a new onboarding process for new hires."* Copilot pulls from your context and generates a structured document with headings, paragraphs, and actionable sections.

For better results, give it more detail. If you already have an outline or bullet points, paste them into the prompt box. Click **Generate** and you have a starting document. You can keep it, regenerate for a different version, or discard it entirely.

:::tip Key Takeaway
Starting from an existing file? Type **/** and select a file from your OneDrive. Copilot builds your new document from that source.
:::

### Rewriting

This is where Copilot gets powerful.

Highlight any paragraph in Word. Click the Copilot icon in the margin. Then say:

- *"Make this more formal."*
- *"Rewrite this for a nontechnical audience."*
- *"Make this shorter and punchier."*

Copilot gives you **multiple options**. Pick the one you like, or fine-tune it with follow-up prompts.

**Drafting and rewriting are different.** Drafting creates new content from nothing. Rewriting changes the tone, clarity, or length of what you already wrote. One builds from zero. The other polishes what exists.

:::info Think About It
What is the practical difference between drafting new content and rewriting existing text in Word?

*Drafting creates from nothing; rewriting changes the tone, clarity, or length of existing text.*
:::

### Summarizing

Got a 20-page report and need the three key takeaways? Copilot creates a document summary in one click.

Or summarize specific sections. Highlight a long paragraph, click Copilot, and type *"Give me the key takeaways in three bullets."* It distills everything down.

## Try It Today

You know what Copilot can do. Here are the fastest ways to get value.

### Excel Quick Wins

1. **Analyze this data** — Click the Copilot button, type *"What insights can you find in this data?"* Copilot surfaces trends, outliers, and patterns you may have missed.
2. **What formula do I need** — Stuck on calculations? Ask in plain English: *"How do I calculate the average sales by region?"* It writes and explains the formula.
3. **Create a chart** — *"Show monthly revenue as a bar chart."* Done. Editable and linked to your data.

### Word Quick Wins

1. **Draft a professional email** — *"Write a follow-up email to a client after our product demo."* First draft, thirty seconds.
2. **Rewrite a paragraph** — Highlight any rough text. Click Copilot. *"Make this clearer and more concise."* Choose the best version.
3. **Summarize a report** — Click Copilot and ask for a summary. Or pick a section for targeted takeaways.

A quick win is a one-line prompt that delivers immediate value. A complex workflow takes more setup and time. Start with quick wins. Build confidence. Then explore more advanced use cases.
