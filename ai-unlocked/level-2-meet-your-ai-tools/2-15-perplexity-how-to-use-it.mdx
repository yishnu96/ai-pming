## When to Reach for It

Not every question needs Perplexity. But when you need it, nothing else works the same way.

**Use Perplexity when:**
- You need current information with sources — "What are the latest remote work tax rules in 2026?"
- You're researching competitors — "How does Notion's pricing compare to Coda?"
- You're comparing products — "Best project management tools for teams under 20 people"
- You need to understand a trending topic — "What changed in GDPR enforcement this year?"

**Use Google instead when:**
- You need simple lookups — "What time is it in Berlin?" or "What does 'churn rate' mean?"
- You're searching for local businesses — "Coffee shops near me"

**Use ChatGPT instead when:**
- You need creative writing — emails, blog drafts, social media posts
- You're brainstorming — "Give me 10 product naming ideas"
- You're drafting or editing documents — summaries, rewrites, tone adjustments

The difference is simple: ChatGPT thinks from memory. Perplexity searches the live web and shows you its receipts.

## Organizing Research

You found a great answer. Now what? Perplexity has a hidden power feature for that.

It's called **Spaces**. A Space is a folder that saves all your searches on one topic so you never lose a research thread.

Here's how people actually use them:
- A "Competitor Research" Space — every search you run about rival products lives here
- A "Vendor Evaluation" Space — tools you're considering for your team, with pros and cons
- A "Market Trends" Space — industry shifts, regulations, and news you want to track

Every Space remembers your search history. You can also set custom instructions for each one. Tell your "Competitor Research" Space to always focus on pricing and feature comparisons. Tell your "Vendor Evaluation" Space to prioritize security and integrations.

## Don't Trust Blindly

Citations don't mean correct. Here is how to verify without losing the speed benefit.

Perplexity gives you sources. That's better than most AI tools. But it can still get things wrong. The difference is that Perplexity makes it easier to catch mistakes.

**Always click the numbered citations.** Make sure the source actually supports what Perplexity claims. Sometimes the AI misreads or over-extrapolates from a source.

**Cross-check important facts with a second source.** If Perplexity says "72% of companies use AI" and cites one blog post, find the original research and verify the number yourself.

**Perplexity can still hallucinate.** It just gives you the tools to catch it faster. Most AI tools give you confidence without proof. Perplexity gives you proof — but the proof needs checking.

The golden rule: never publish AI output without verification.

:::warning Common Mistake
Assuming a citation means the claim is correct. Always open the source and read it yourself before relying on statistics or claims from Perplexity.
:::

:::info Think About It
If Perplexity cited a source you've never heard of, would you click it before using that stat in a presentation?

*The answer should always be yes. One unchecked stat can destroy your credibility.*
:::

That's the complete Perplexity workflow: ask, verify, organize.
