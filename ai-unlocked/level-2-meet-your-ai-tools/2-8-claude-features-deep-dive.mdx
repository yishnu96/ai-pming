## Your Workspace

Think of a regular Claude chat like a phone call. You talk, it answers. You hang up, it forgets.

A **Project** in Claude is different. It is like an office with its own files, its own rules, and its own memory. Each project gets a dedicated workspace with a built-in knowledge base and custom instructions. You upload documents, PDFs, spreadsheets — anything Claude should know about before you even start typing.

Say you are running a product launch. Instead of pasting your product requirements document every single time, you create a project called "Launch Q3," drop the PRD inside, and Claude reads it automatically. Every conversation in that project starts from the same baseline.

Here is what makes this powerful for product managers: every conversation in the project carries the same context. You do not need to re-explain your brand guidelines, your timeline, or your constraints. Claude already knows.

## Side-by-Side Output

Every other AI tool buries its best work in the chat stream. Tables scroll past. Code blocks hide halfway up the page. You lose track of what you asked for five minutes ago.

Claude puts it next door.

**Artifacts** are a separate panel that sits beside your conversation. Think of it this way: the chat is your phone call with Claude. Artifacts are the whiteboard it sketches on while you talk.

When Claude generates a table, a document draft, a diagram, or code, it opens in the artifact panel. You can preview it, copy it, or edit it without the chat getting cluttered. Code artifacts even preview as live components so you can see what they produce before using them.

This matters because you are not reading through a wall of text to find the thing you actually need. The deliverable lives in its own space, clear and separate.

## Claude Remembers

<Star3 color="#06D6A0" stroke="black" strokeWidth={2} size={32} />

Imagine telling someone your brand guidelines once — and every future interaction, they just know. No reminders. No copy-pasting. They remember.

That is what **Memory** does in Claude.

Claude can remember facts about you, your preferences, and your project — but only within the project you set it up in. It does not carry information across your entire account. A "Marketing" project knows marketing stuff. A "Budget" project knows budget stuff. They do not mix unless you want them to.

There is also a memory toggle. You can turn memory on or off for any project, or clear it entirely. Your privacy stays in your hands.

This connects to something called the Zeigarnik effect — a quirk of human psychology where our brains hold onto unfinished tasks. Claude's memory does something similar: it keeps your project context alive between sessions so you never have to re-set the stage.

## Make It Work For You

Here is how all three features work together for a real product manager:

1. **Organize with Projects.** Create a "Feature Launch Q3" project. Upload your PRD, competitive analysis, and stakeholder notes. Set a custom instruction: "Always flag risks and dependencies."
2. **Deliver with Artifacts.** Ask Claude to draft a launch timeline. It opens as an artifact — a clean table you can preview, copy, or paste straight into your planning doc.
3. **Remember with Memory.** Over weeks, Claude learns your launch cadence, your preferred communication style, your risk appetite. It gets tuned to how you work.

The decision framework is simple:

- Need to **organize** your work around a topic? Use Projects.
- Need to **deliver** something you can see and use? Claude creates Artifacts.
- Need Claude to **remember** context between sessions? Turn on Memory.

Claude stops being a chat window. It becomes your workspace.
