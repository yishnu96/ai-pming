## Right Where You Work

Here's what Gemini actually does inside your Google apps:

- **Docs** — "Help me write" lets you draft, rewrite, or brainstorm without leaving the document.
- **Gmail** — Gemini reads your email threads and writes summaries. It also helps you compose replies.
- **Sheets** — Type a formula in plain English. Gemini writes it. You can also ask it to create charts from your data.
- **Slides** — Give it text, and it generates a full deck.

There's a March 2026 update that makes this even more powerful: one prompt can now pull content from multiple Google apps and create a Doc, Sheet, or Slide. One instruction, multiple apps working together.

## When Gemini Wins

<Star2 color="#06D6A0" stroke="black" strokeWidth={2} size={28} />

Why does Gemini beat a standalone AI tool for certain tasks? Simple: **"It already has my stuff."**

When you're looking at an email in Gmail, Gemini already sees it. You don't need to copy-paste. A standalone AI needs a paste. Gemini doesn't.

Three wins that make Gemini the obvious choice:

1. **Already in your apps** — No switching tabs, no new URLs to bookmark.
2. **Reads your data natively** — Gemini can pull from your Drive, your calendar, your emails. No upload required.
3. **Output is formatted by default** — Gemini drafts directly into a Doc. It builds directly into a Slide. You're not fixing formatting after pasting AI output.

## What It Can't Do Yet

Just because it's in your apps doesn't mean it's the smartest tool in the room.

Gemini in Workspace has real limitations today:

- **Less nuance than Claude or GPT-4** — Complex strategic thinking, deep analysis, and creative reasoning still favor standalone AI models.
- **Fewer customization options** — You can't set temperature controls, upload custom knowledge bases, or fine-tune its behavior the way you can with ChatGPT or Claude.
- **Tied to Google** — If your company uses Microsoft 365, Gemini in Google Workspace is irrelevant. It's locked to the Google ecosystem.
- **Sometimes generic output** — When asked open-ended questions, Gemini can produce templated, cookie-cutter responses. Standalone AI with good prompting gives sharper, more specific results.

The trade-off is clear: **convenience versus capability**. Gemini trades some depth for seamless access to your work.

## Your Gemini Playbook

Use this decision guide:

- **"In a Google app + uses my data?"** → Gemini.
- **"Deep thinking or complex analysis needed?"** → Standalone AI like Claude or ChatGPT.

Match it to your day: drafting an email from an existing thread? Gemini. Writing a strategy document from scratch? Standalone AI. Building a slide deck from notes you already have? Gemini.
