## The Three Claudes

Imagine three coworkers sitting at the same desk.

One types at lightning speed and handles quick requests without breaking a sweat. Another is your everyday reliable teammate --- solid at almost anything you throw at them. The third is the deep thinker who needs a quiet room for an hour but comes back with insights nobody else saw.

That's Claude in a nutshell. Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus are not "good, better, best." They're "fast, balanced, and deep." Picking the wrong one doesn't break anything --- but it wastes your time and money.

## Meet Haiku

You've been there. You type a simple question into an AI tool and wait. And wait. Fifteen seconds later, the answer appears. The answer was fine. But the wait wasn't.

That's what Claude Haiku is built to fix.

Haiku is the sprinter of the family. It handles quick tasks without the heavy lifting. Rewriting an email. Summarizing a short memo. Sorting a list. Categorizing customer feedback. Tasks that don't require deep reasoning --- just speed and accuracy.

Think of it like this: you don't call an electrician to change a lightbulb. Haiku is the same idea. Use it when the job is straightforward and you want an answer now.

**What Haiku is NOT:** A replacement for Sonnet or Opus on complex work. If you ask Haiku to analyze a 50-page strategy document, it will try --- but you'll get a surface-level answer that misses nuance. That's not Haiku's job.

:::info Think About It

What's one repetitive, quick task you do at least once a day that an AI could handle in seconds instead of minutes?

:::

Here's the open question: if Haiku handles the small stuff, and Opus handles the heavy lifting --- what about everything in between?

## Meet Sonnet

You're probably using Sonnet right now and didn't even know it.

Sonnet is Claude's daily driver --- the model built for the work you actually do. Writing project briefs. Drafting emails that sound like you, not a robot. Summarizing meeting notes. Reviewing documents for key points. Analyzing customer feedback and pulling patterns.

Sonnet hits the sweet spot. It's fast enough for everyday tasks and smart enough for work that requires some thought. Most professionals will use Sonnet 80 percent of the time and barely notice the other models.

**What Sonnet is NOT:** The answer to every problem. When you need genuine strategic thinking --- like comparing two competing business models or writing a creative campaign from scratch --- Sonnet will give you a decent answer. But it won't give you the _best_ answer.

:::info Think About It

Think about the last document you drafted. How much time did you spend getting the tone right? What if Sonnet could nail it on the first try?

:::

But some problems need more than "good enough." That's where Opus comes in.

## Meet Opus

What if an AI could literally show you how it thought through a problem, step by step?

That's Opus. And that's exactly what it does with a feature called **Extended Thinking** --- you can literally watch Opus reason through your question in real time, like watching someone work a math problem on a whiteboard.

Opus is the deep specialist. Complex reasoning. Strategic analysis. Creative writing that reads like a human wrote it. Comparing two lengthy documents and finding contradictions. Brainstorming naming options for a new product. Working through a business decision where the details matter.

Using Opus to rewrite a two-sentence email is like paying for a Ferrari to pick up groceries. It will do the job beautifully. It's just overkill.

**What Opus is NOT:** A faster version of Sonnet. Opus actually takes longer to respond because it's thinking deeper. If you need speed, Opus frustrates you. If you need depth, Opus delivers.

:::info Think About It

What's one decision you've put off because it required comparing too many details? Could you hand that to Opus and get a structured breakdown?

:::

Now you know all three models. So how do you actually decide which one to use on a Tuesday morning when you just need to get things done?

## How to Pick

Here's the rule of thumb:

- **Quick and simple?** Haiku. Rewriting emails, summarizing short notes, sorting data.
- **Daily work?** Sonnet. Drafting documents, analyzing mid-length content, writing with the right tone.
- **Needs real thinking?** Opus. Complex analysis, creative work, strategic decisions.

Your plan determines what you get access to. The free tier gives limited access to Sonnet. The Pro plan ($20/month) unlocks both Sonnet and Opus. Teams and Enterprise get everything with higher usage limits.

## Try This Now

Here's a test you can run in under five minutes.

1. Go to claude.ai and sign up for free if you haven't already.
2. Upload any PDF --- a report, article, or document you're working with.
3. Ask: **"Summarize the key points in three bullet points."**
4. Then paste the same document into ChatGPT and ask the same question.
5. Compare the two answers. Which one captures the nuance better? Which one would you actually use in a meeting?

That difference is the gap between a tool that talks and a tool that understands.
