## Your Daily Driver (GPT-4o)

That moment you need an AI that just works without thinking about it.

**GPT-4o** is ChatGPT's flagship model. The "o" stands for "omni" --- because it handles text, images, and audio all at once. Think of it like a Swiss Army knife: not the absolute best at any single thing, but reliably good at almost everything.

Use GPT-4o when you need something done well without overthinking it. Drafting an email? Planning a trip? Analyzing a screenshot of a spreadsheet? GPT-4o handles all of these smoothly. It's the model that's selected by default when you open ChatGPT, and for good reason.

**Here's what it's not:** It's not the fastest option, and it's not the cheapest to run. If you're asking a basic question that needs a one-line answer, GPT-4o is overkill --- though you likely won't notice the difference on the free plan.

:::info Think About It
What's one task you do in ChatGPT almost every day? Is GPT-4o the right fit --- or could you get away with something lighter?
:::

## Quick Answers (GPT-4o Mini)

Why use a sledgehammer when a thumbtack works faster?

**GPT-4o Mini** is the lightweight sibling of GPT-4o. It's built for speed and simplicity. Ask it a straightforward question, and it fires back an answer almost instantly.

Picture this: you're in a meeting and someone asks, "What's the difference between ROI and ROAS?" You pull up ChatGPT, type the question, and GPT-4o Mini gives you a clear, two-sentence answer in under a second. No elaborate reasoning. No deep analysis. Just the answer you need, fast.

**Here's what it's not:** It won't handle complex strategy questions well. Ask it to design a six-month product roadmap, and you'll get something that looks right on the surface but misses the nuance. GPT-4o Mini trades depth for speed.

:::info Think About It
Next time you have a simple "what is this?" or "define this" question in mind, would GPT-4o Mini be enough instead of the full model?
:::

### Hard Problems (o1 and o3)
</div>

These models pause before answering. Yes, really --- they think first.

**o1** and **o3** are OpenAI's reasoning models. Unlike GPT-4o, which streams answers word by word as it goes, these models do something unusual: they "think" before they respond. You'll actually see a progress bar or thinking indicator while they work through the problem internally.

Think of the difference like this: GPT-4o is like a chef who starts cooking as soon as you order. o1 and o3 are like chefs who step back, consider all your preferences, plan the entire dish, and then cook. The food takes longer, but the result is more carefully constructed.

Use o1 or o3 when the problem genuinely demands reasoning. Working through a complex logic puzzle? Comparing business strategies with multiple tradeoffs? Writing code that needs to be correct the first time? These models excel where step-by-step thinking matters.

**Here's what they're not:** They're not for everyday tasks. Asking o1 what time a meeting starts is like calling a consultant to ask what's for lunch. They're slower by design, and they cost more to run.

:::info Think About It
Can you recall a time when ChatGPT gave you an answer that sounded right but was actually wrong? Could a reasoning model have caught that mistake with more careful thinking?
:::

## Real Research (Deep Research)

One button. Ten minutes. Dozens of sources.

**Deep Research** is not a model. It's a mode. When you activate it in ChatGPT, the AI doesn't just answer from what it already knows. It goes out, searches the web, reads multiple sources, cross-checks facts, and compiles a research report with citations --- all automatically.

Imagine you need to understand the competitive landscape for AI writing tools in 2026. Instead of opening twenty tabs yourself, you click "Deep Research," type your question, and come back ten minutes later to a structured report with linked sources, key findings, and a summary of what different experts are saying.

**Here's what it's not:** It's not a quick answer. It takes several minutes to complete. Do not use it for simple questions --- it's designed for multi-step research tasks that would normally take you hours.

:::info Think About It
What research task are you putting off right now because it feels too time-consuming to dig through sources yourself?
:::

## Pick Your Model

Here's a simple framework. Before opening ChatGPT, ask yourself: what kind of task is this?

1. **Simple fact or quick question?** Use GPT-4o Mini --- fast and direct.
2. **Everyday writing, planning, or analysis?** Use GPT-4o --- balanced and reliable.
3. **Complex reasoning or strategy?** Use o1 or o3 --- thorough and careful.
4. **Multi-source research with citations?** Use Deep Research --- comprehensive and cited.

### Try This Now

Pause five seconds before you open ChatGPT. Ask yourself: what kind of task is this --- quick, daily, complex, or research? Pick the model first, then type your question.

Do this for one day. You'll notice the answers getting better not because you changed your prompts, but because you finally picked the right model for the job.
