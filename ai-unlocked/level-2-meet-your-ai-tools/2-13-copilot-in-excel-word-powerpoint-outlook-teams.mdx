In the last article, we met Copilot's two levels -- free and paid. Now let's open the hood and see what the paid version actually does inside the programs you use every day. Because that's where the magic lives.

## Copilot in Excel

What if you could just ask Excel to create the formula for you -- in plain English?

That's exactly what Copilot does. Instead of wrestling with `=IF(VLOOKUP(...))` nightmares, you type: "Highlight all sales over $10,000 and calculate the average." Copilot writes the formula and applies it. You never needed to memorize Excel's 400+ functions.

But it goes further. Click the **"Analyze this data"** button and Copilot reads your entire spreadsheet. It spots trends you might have missed -- "Sales peaked in March," "Region B underperformed by 12 percent." Then it builds charts automatically. You pick the visual, it does the math.

**What it is NOT:** Copilot does not replace your judgment. It suggests formulas and spots patterns, but you still decide which insights matter. Think of it as a brilliant assistant -- not the manager.

## Copilot in Word

You have a blank page and a deadline. Copilot starts the page for you.

Click **"Draft with Copilot"** and type a rough idea -- "Meeting agenda for Q1 planning" or "Thank you note to our design team." Within seconds, you have a structured document with headings, bullet points, and actual content. Not perfect, but a real first draft. And first drafts are the hardest part.

From there, the editing becomes effortless:

- **"Rewrite this"** -- Make a paragraph clearer, shorter, or more professional. Copilot preserves your meaning while cleaning up the language.
- **"Summarize this"** -- Got a 15-page report? Copilot condenses it to three paragraphs so you know what matters before you invest an hour reading.
- **"Change the tone"** -- Turn a blunt draft into something diplomatic. Or make a casual note sound executive-ready.

**The reality check:** Copilot drafts are starting points, not final products. The first output will feel generic -- because it doesn't know your voice yet. That's why the rewrite and tone features matter more than the initial draft. You shape, it writes, you shape again.

:::info Think About It

What's one document you've been putting off because the blank page feels intimidating? Copilot fills that blank space so you have something to edit instead of nothing to stare at.

:::

The gap between "I should write this" and "I'm editing this" just got a lot smaller.

## PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams

Three quick wins. One app at a time.

**PowerPoint:** Give Copilot a Word document or a text prompt, and it generates entire slide decks. Not just bullet points -- layouts, speaker notes, suggested images. For example, paste your quarterly report into Copilot and say "Make this into 10 slides." It structures the narrative for you. You tweak the design.

**Outlook:** You open your inbox and see a 30-email thread. Instead of scrolling through every reply, Copilot summarizes: "Sarah asked for budget approval. Tom pushed back on timeline. You need to respond by Friday." It can also draft replies that match your writing style.

**Teams:** Missed a meeting? Click Copilot and ask "What did I miss?" It produces a summary with decisions made, who said what, and which actions are assigned to you. No more asking colleagues to catch you up.

**What these apps share:** Copilot eliminates the busywork. Not the thinking -- the shuffling. Reading, summarizing, drafting the first version. Those tasks are now one click away.

:::info Think About It

How many hours this week did you spend reading long threads, catching up on meetings, or building slides from scratch? Even reclaiming half of that time changes your week.

:::

The real question is whether paying extra for this is worth it for you.

## Worth the Upgrade?

Not everyone should pay for Copilot. Here's who should and who shouldn't.

**Copilot Pro** costs about $20 a month for individuals. **Microsoft 365 Copilot** runs around $30 per user per month for businesses. Both give you the in-app features we just covered.

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>

| Plan | Price | For | In-app features |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Copilot Pro** | ~$20/month | Individuals | Excel, Word, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams |
| **Microsoft 365 Copilot** | ~$30/user/month | Businesses | Same + admin controls and data governance |

</div>

:::tip Key Takeaway

The free version is enough for most beginners. The paid version earns its keep when the apps you use daily become noticeably faster.

:::

Upgrade if:
- You spend hours in Excel every week managing data
- You sit through multiple meetings and struggle to track action items
- You draft long documents regularly -- reports, proposals, briefs
- Your team shares heavy email threads and you're always playing catch-up

Skip it if:
- You use Office apps casually -- a few emails here and there, basic documents
- Your main need is answering questions or summarizing web pages -- the free Copilot in Edge handles that perfectly

## Try This Now

You don't need a paid Copilot to feel the benefit today.

1. Open **Microsoft Edge** (or download it free)
2. Click the **Copilot icon** in the top-right corner
3. Open any long article or report
4. Type: **"Summarize this page"**

Watch how fast Copilot extracts the key points. That small moment -- getting the essence of a long page in seconds -- is the same technology that powers Copilot inside Word, Excel, and the rest of Office. The engine is identical. The difference is whether it can read your files or just the web.
