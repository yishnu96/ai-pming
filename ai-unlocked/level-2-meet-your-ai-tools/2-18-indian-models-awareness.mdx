## They Exist

You've heard of ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini. But India is building its own AI — and it's built for a very different audience.

Western AI models struggle with Indian languages. Try asking ChatGPT a question in Tamil or Bengali, and you'll often get broken grammar or a polite switch to English. Indian AI models fix that gap. They're designed for India's unique landscape: 22 official languages, hundreds of dialects, and cultural context that no Silicon Valley model captures.

A key government initiative driving this is **Bhashini** — India's national language AI platform. It translates between 22 Indian languages and powers everything from government services to startup apps.

:::tip Why This Matters
If you work with Indian audiences, customers, or content, these models understand the context Western AI misses.
:::

:::info Think About It

How often do you switch between English and your native language when talking to colleagues or customers? Now imagine an AI that does the same.
:::

## Krutrim

India's answer to the AI race.

**Krutrim** is created by Ola (yes, the ride-hailing company). It was India's first major commercial AI launch and positioned as "India's own AI."

**What it does well:**

- Understands Indian cultural context — local holidays, business practices, regional references
- Works with Indian languages better than most Western models
- Handles local knowledge: pricing in rupees, Indian geography, local regulations

**Where it falls short:**

- Less polished than GPT-4 or Claude
- Smaller model, so complex reasoning can stumble
- Limited global knowledge compared to established competitors

**Try this prompt:** Ask it about the best time to visit Rajasthan, or request a business email in Hinglish. Watch how it handles the mix of English and Hindi.

:::info Think About It

If you needed an AI to help draft content for an Indian audience, would Krutrim's local knowledge give it an edge — or would you stick with what you know?
:::

*But Krutrim isn't the only one. There's another player going after enterprise-grade Indian AI.*

## Sarvam AI

Where Indian AI gets serious.

**Sarvam AI** built two models: Sarvam-30B and Sarvam-105B. These aren't small experiments — they're models built specifically for Indian languages and enterprise use.

**What sets Sarvam apart:**

- Strong at **code-switching** — when you mix English with Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, or other languages mid-sentence. This is how millions of Indians actually communicate.
- Designed for business: customer service bots, document processing, multilingual content creation
- Backed by government visibility at the Delhi AI Summit 2026

**Where it stands today:** Promising but early. It doesn't have the breadth of features that Western alternatives offer yet. Think of it as a specialized tool, not a full replacement.

:::info Think About It

Code-switching is how real conversations happen in India. An AI that understands "Can you send me the report by shaam?" has an advantage over one that doesn't.
:::

*So far, we've looked at commercial AI. But India also built something the public can use — for one specific job.*

## Bhashini

A language translator built for a billion people.

**Bhashini** is the Indian government's AI translation platform. It translates between 22 Indian languages through an open API that any developer can use.

**What it's most useful for:**

- Translating official documents between languages
- Government communications across India's linguistic diversity
- Multilingual content for businesses and apps

**What it's not:** Bhashini is not a general AI chatbot. It won't write your emails or brainstorm ideas. It does one thing — translate languages — and it does it for all of India.

:::info Think About It

Imagine a farmer in rural Maharashtra reading a government notice in Marathi, while the same notice was originally written in Assamese. That's what Bhashini enables.
:::
