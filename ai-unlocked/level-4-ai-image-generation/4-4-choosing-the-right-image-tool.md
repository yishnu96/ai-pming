# Choosing the Right Image Tool

You need a product photo for your online store. A banner for a LinkedIn post. A mockup for a client pitch. Three different needs — and each one points to a different AI image tool. Pick wrong and you waste money, time, or both. Pick right and you get professional visuals in minutes.

This guide helps you make that call.

## Free vs. Paid at a Glance

| Tool | Best For | Free Plan | Paid From | Commercial Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **ChatGPT (GPT Image)** | General use, text in images | Yes (limited) | $20/mo (Plus) | Yes |
| **Adobe Firefly** | Commercial-safe design work | Yes (limited) | $9.99/mo | Yes, IP-indemnified |
| **Midjourney V7** | Artistic quality, concepts | No | $10/mo | Yes (paid plans) |
| **Canva AI** | Social posts, presentations | Yes (limited) | $15/mo (Pro) | Yes |
| **Ideogram 3.0** | Text, logos, typography | 25 images/mo | $8/mo | Yes |
| **Leonardo AI** | Business and game assets | 150 tokens/day | $12/mo | Yes |
| **Stable Diffusion 3.5** | Full control, no limits | Free (open-source) | Free (self-hosted) | Yes |

:::info Key Takeaway

Paid tools give you volume, speed, and commercial rights. Free plans are great for testing and occasional use — they come with daily limits, watermarks, or lower resolution.

:::

## Which Tool for Which Need

### Presentations

**Winner: Canva AI**

Canva generates images inside a complete design platform. You get the image, the slide template, the right dimensions, and export — all in one workflow.

A marketing manager at a mid-size company does not want to generate an image, download it, open PowerPoint, resize it, and fix the aspect ratio. Canva compresses all of that into a single step.

Adobe Firefly is a close second if the team already uses Creative Cloud. Firefly lives inside Photoshop and Adobe Express, so the handoff is seamless.

### Social Media

**Winner: Canva AI or Midjourney**

For social media managers who need volume and variety — different sizes for Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook — Canva AI wins on workflow. Generate once, publish everywhere.

When the goal is a visually striking post that stops the scroll — a fantasy-style brand illustration, a moody product shot — Midjourney V7 produces the most eye-catching output. It starts at $10/month and includes commercial rights on every paid plan.

:::tip Real-World Example

A freelance social media manager uses Midjourney to create unique hero visuals for three clients ($30/mo plan), then drops them into Canva for formatting. Two tools, perfect results.

:::

### Mockups and Product Photography

**Winner: ChatGPT (GPT Image) or Ideogram**

Need a smartphone mockup on a wooden desk, or a t-shirt with your logo displayed as a flat-lay photo? ChatGPT handles these "product in context" prompts reliably because it understands complex instructions through natural conversation.

When your mockup needs visible text — a storefront sign, a branded coffee cup, a book cover — Ideogram 3.0 is unmatched. It renders readable, properly styled text directly inside generated images. Starting at $8/month, it is the most affordable dedicated tool on this list.

### E-Commerce and Volume Needs

**Winner: Leonardo AI**

For online stores generating hundreds of product variants, backgrounds, or lifestyle shots — Leonardo AI token model and business-focused features make it the top choice. The free tier gives 150 tokens per day, and paid plans scale to 60,000 tokens at $60/month.

## Commercial Usage Rights

This is where choices get serious. Using an AI-generated image on a client website, in an ad campaign, or on merchandise carries legal risk if you do not understand the license.

### What You Need to Know

| Platform | Commercial Rights on Free Plan | Commercial Rights on Paid Plan | IP Indemnification |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ChatGPT (GPT Image) | Limited | Yes | No |
| Adobe Firefly | Limited | Yes | **Yes** |
| Midjourney | No | Yes | No |
| Canva AI | Limited | Yes | No |
| Ideogram | Limited | Yes | No |
| Leonardo AI | Yes (with attribution) | Yes | No |
| Stable Diffusion | Yes (open license) | Yes | No |

:::warning Important

Adobe Firefly is the only major tool that offers IP indemnification. This means if someone claims the AI-generated content infringes their copyright, Adobe covers the legal liability. For agency work or large brands, this is the deciding factor.

:::

### Rules of Thumb

- **Always check current terms.** Licensing terms change. What was free commercial use last year may have changed.
- **Free plan means limited rights.** Most platforms restrict commercial use to paid subscribers. Do not assume free means free for business.
- **Label AI-generated content where required.** Some platforms and jurisdictions require disclosure.
- **Your prompt matters.** If you generate an image that closely resembles an existing trademarked character or logo, the platform license will not protect you.

:::tip When in Doubt, Use Firefly

For any client work, brand asset, or commercial publication — Adobe Firefly is the safest choice. It is trained only on licensed content, and the IP indemnification gives you a legal safety net no other tool provides.

:::

## Try This Now

1. **Pick one task** you have coming up this week — a social post, a presentation slide, or a product image.
2. **Try the free plan** of the recommended tool from the tables above. Spend 15 minutes generating images.
3. **Compare two tools.** Generate the same prompt in ChatGPT and in Canva AI. Note which gives you better results for your specific need.
4. **Check the terms.** Visit the licensing page of whichever tool you choose. Confirm commercial rights before using any image in a real project.
